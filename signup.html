Important Notes & Rules

Who Can Register a Marriage

  • The bride must be at least 18 years old and the groom at least 21 years old
  • Both parties must give free and voluntary consent
  • Neither party should have a living spouse (unmarried, divorced or widowed with proof)
  • Marriage must be solemnized according to religious rites or under the Special Marriage Act

Child Marriage Prohibition

Child marriage is strictly prohibited under the Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006. The legal consequences include:

  • Imprisonment up to 2 years and/or fine up to ₹1 lakh for performing, conducting or promoting child marriage
  • Child marriages are voidable at the option of the minor party
  • Any marriage where either party is below legal age is considered child marriage
  • Report child marriages to Childline at 1098 or local authorities

Required Documents

Identity Proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID/Passport)
Age Proof (Birth Certificate/School Certificate)
Address Proof (Utility Bill/Driving License)
Marriage Proof (Invitation/Photos)
Passport Size Photos (4 each)
Affidavits of Marital Status
Witness Details with ID Proof
Previous Marriage Termination Proof (if applicable)

Registration Timeline & Fees

  • Registration within 90 days: ₹100-150 fee
  • Registration after 90 days but within 150 days: Higher late fee applies
  • Certificate issued within 7-15 working days after verification
  • For Special Marriage Act: 30-day notice period required
